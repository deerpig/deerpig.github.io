The Blue Brain Project; reverse engineering the brain.

Posted on 05 Feb 2010 by Brad Collins

>
--- layout: post title: "The Blue Brain Project; reverse engineering the brain." topics: computational neuroscience style: blog ---

Found this video yesterday via Boing Boing. The Blue Brain Project is an attempt to create a synthetic brain by reverse-engineering the mammalian brain down to the molecular level. They hope to have a complete model of a rat brain within two years, a cat brain two years after that and then on to a primate brain before building a human brain within the next ten years.

While the Blue Brain Project is very distant from what we are building, it struck a chord as being in tune with the models we are basing BMF Chunkspaces on. Over the last few weeks I've begun thinking about the idea of chunk spaces in terms of a kind of archival epistemology. In otherwords, I'm finding that it's useful, as our chunkspace model is being developed to ask, what a BMF Bramble knows.

There are many who see the Blue Brain Project as little more than an impossible and expensive Quixotic quest. Perhaps, but no matter what the outcome, the project is inspiring and nothing short of breathtaking in its scope and ambition.

If the video doesn't blow your mind, seek help.
