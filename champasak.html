Champasak Province

Champasak is a province in southwestern Laos, near the borders with Thailand and Cambodia. The Mekong river flows through the province. The small town of Champasak, is home to the Khmer ruins of Wat Phu a United Nations World Heritage Site.

Early History

In the early AD 600's, Chenla was a vassal state of the Kingdom of Funan which, over a 60 year period achieved its independence and eventually conquering all of Funan. The capital of the new empire was Isanapura (located in present day Cambodia) but later divided into northern and southern states known as "Chenla of the Land" and "Chenla of the Sea." Modern day Champasak province was the center of the northern state. Chenla was the first of the great Khmer empires which later established the well known temple complex at Ankhor Wat in central Cambodia.

In the fourteenth century Champasak became one of the three principalities that succeeded the Lao kingdom of Lan Xang. Champasak broke away from Lan Xang in 1713 to become the Kingdom of Champasak which lasted until it was abolished in 1946 when the Kingdom of Laos was formed.

Vietnamese Conflict

Laos was part of the Ho Chi Minh trail which supplied the Vietcong who were fighting an American backed government in south Vietnam. Subsequently, most of Laos was heavily bombed and sprayed with defoliants like Agent Orange throughout the conflict. More ordnance was dropped on Laos than all munitions dropped on Europe during World War II.

Today Laos is still suffering the long term effects of Agent Orange and a staggering amount of UXO (unexploded ordnance) which range from 2,000 pound bombs down to very small cluster munitions (which Lao people call "bombies") is still scattered throughout the country, posing a very real danger to everyone in the country.

Bolaven Plateau Bauxite Mine

The Bolaven Plateau is an elevated region in Champasak province between the Annamite Moutain Range which runs along the eastern border with Vietnam and the Mekong River to the West. The plateau's elevation ranges from about 1,000 to 1,350 meters above sea level.

In recent years, it was discovered that there were enormous bauxite deposits in the sandstone that makes up the plateau.

In 2009-2010 a joint venture company between the Australian Ord River Resources and and the Chinese China Nonferrous Metals International Mining, signed an agreement with the Laos government to establish a 145 square km bauxite mine on the Bolaven Plateau.

The mine is expected to eventually produce up to 2 million tons of Alumina per year. A hydro-electric plant will be built to smelt the alumina from bauxite, and a rail link, (part of the TAR, the United Nations Trans-Asian Railroad) will connect Laos with central Vietnam.

The mine will provide Champasak with jobs, electricity and potentially a new route for a high speed Internet link that connects Laos to the new submarine telecommunications cable landing established in 2009 in central Vietnam.
